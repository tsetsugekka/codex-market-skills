## Purpose

This skill's default output ranks current theme strength. This reference helps answer the second question: is the strong theme likely a durable mainline, a short rotation, an old-leader rebound, a defensive substitute, or one-day noise?

Do not force a lifecycle label when evidence is thin. Write `周期位置暂不确认` rather than inventing a phase.

## From Hot Theme To Mainline

A theme is not a mainline just because it ranks high for one session. Treat `mainline` as a theme that can repeatedly attract capital because it represents a structural economic, industry, or policy transition.

Use three layers:

- `Micro`: representative stocks show orders, earnings, product cycle, cost improvement, capacity utilization, or technology validation.
- `Meso`: the industry chain has broad beneficiaries, supplier/customer confirmation, better pricing, localization, concentration, or capex expansion.
- `Macro`: the theme fits a larger policy, technology, energy, security, productivity, or demographic direction.

The stronger all three layers align, the more likely the theme is a real mainline rather than a one-day rotation.

## Mainline Quality Checklist

A theme is closer to a real mainline when it shows:

- structural demand or policy direction that can last beyond a one-day tape;
- adoption, penetration, order, earnings, capex, or price-increase evidence;
- repeated capital return across sessions;
- leaders making progress while followers also broaden;
- positive contribution from multiple representative constituents, not only one idiosyncratic stock;
- acceptance after pullbacks and no immediate price rejection after good news;
- a clear expectation gap: the market had not fully priced the catalyst before the move.

A theme is more likely noise or a temporary rotation when it shows:

- high return from one stock while peers are flat or weak;
- only 股吧 speculation or stale headlines;
- no sector-level news, order, policy, or earnings evidence;
- large intraday spikes with poor close or frequent failed boards;
- strength only because the previous mainline paused;
- defensive behavior during broad de-risking rather than offensive leadership;
- good news that no longer generates breadth or price progress.

## Penetration And Adoption S-Curve

For industry or technology themes, use adoption/penetration as a lifecycle proxy when reliable data exists:

- `0%-5%`: concept or recognition stage. Narrative uncertainty is high; price can move before fundamentals are visible.
- `5%-35%`: acceleration stage. This is often the best zone for mainline expansion because adoption, earnings, valuation, and capital inflow can reinforce each other.
- `35%-80%`: maturation stage. Growth may continue, but valuation expansion usually becomes harder and stock selection matters more.
- `80%+`: saturation stage. The market needs profit quality, concentration, cash return, overseas expansion, or productivity gains; pure penetration logic is mostly exhausted.

Do not fabricate penetration data. If no reliable penetration/adoption data is available, use price breadth, leader/follower behavior, news quality, and fund-flow evidence instead.

## Feedback Loops

Healthy mainline feedback:

```text
theme evidence -> leader strength -> money-making effect -> more followers -> more research/news attention -> more capital return
```

Dangerous late-cycle feedback:

```text
valuation overdraw -> high turnover without progress -> followers fail -> leaders wobble -> discussion becomes one-sided -> capital leaves
```

For TOP3 checks, do not only name the strongest representative stock. Ask whether that stock is pulling the theme, or whether the theme has enough breadth to validate the representative stock.

## Theme Termination Signals

A theme can weaken for three different reasons:

1. `Natural maturity`: adoption slows, valuation stops expanding, and good news creates smaller price response.
2. `Logic invalidation`: policy, technology path, business model, demand, or earnings evidence breaks the original thesis.
3. `Liquidity suffocation`: the story remains valid but incremental money leaves because of risk-off, rates, funding pressure, crowding, issuance, or competing themes.

The response differs:

- Natural maturity: downgrade expected return and avoid treating every rebound as a new mainline.
- Logic invalidation: mark the theme as failed until fresh evidence appears.
- Liquidity suffocation: watch for breadth repair, leader stabilization, and capital return before upgrading again.

## Phase Labels

Use these compact labels in follow-up analysis or optional TOP3 checks:

- `萌芽`: theme appears, discussion rises, leaders resist market weakness, but breadth is still limited.
- `启动`: leaders trend, several peers confirm, news and price begin reinforcing each other.
- `加速`: followers broaden, money-making effect improves, pullbacks are quickly bought.
- `高潮/分歧`: attention is one-sided, turnover rises, leaders wobble, followers fail, or good news stops moving price.
- `退潮`: leaders break, failed-board or亏钱效应 spreads, and capital leaves the theme.
- `旧主线反抽`: former leader rebounds on memory and liquidity but lacks new evidence.
- `防御替代`: cash-flow, resource, healthcare, dividend, or policy-hedge themes rise because offensive themes weaken.
- `噪音`: isolated one-day move without breadth or confirmed driver.

## Driver Check Integration

For each TOP3 theme, add only a short lifecycle sentence when evidence supports it:

```text
周期位置：启动/加速/高潮分歧/旧主线反抽/防御替代/噪音/暂不确认。
```

Then add the reason in one sentence:

```text
判断依据：代表股是否具有主题外溢性、同题材是否扩散、资讯是否指向行业级催化、资金是否反复回流。
```

Keep the default output compact. The ranking answers strength; this lens answers durability.
